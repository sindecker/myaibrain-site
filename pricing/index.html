Questions

Straightforward answers.

Is the free tier actually free? No tricks?
Yes. No credit card, no expiry, no feature lock behind a login wall. 22 workflows and full persistent memory are yours permanently. Pro and Team add more capability on top — they don't take anything away from Free.
Does AIBrain send my data anywhere?
No. Every tier runs completely locally. Your brain, your memories, your agent logs — all stored on your machine. Nothing is transmitted to our servers. AIBrain was built for exactly this: private, local, persistent memory that you own.
What LLMs does AIBrain work with?
AIBrain is agent-agnostic. It works with Claude, GPT-4o, Gemini, Llama, Mistral, and any local model via Ollama or LM Studio. It also integrates with LangChain, CrewAI, LlamaIndex, OpenAI Agents SDK, AutoGen, and 15+ other frameworks.
Can I cancel Pro or Team at any time?
Yes. Cancel from your dashboard at any time. Your subscription runs until the end of the billing period — no fees, no hassle.
What is the "launch price" on Pro?
We're capping the first 200 Pro subscriptions at $9.95/mo as a thank-you to early adopters. Once those 200 spots are taken, pricing moves to standard. Subscribe now to lock in the launch price permanently.
What's the difference between Boss Agent on Pro vs. Team?
Pro gives you a Boss Agent that can orchestrate up to 3 worker agents — great for a personal multi-agent setup. Team scales this to 50 agents with AI Companies (full org chart with CEO, managers, workers), RBAC, goal tracking, and cross-machine sync.
I'm a developer building on top of AIBrain. Which tier is right for me?
Start with Free — the pip package and API are fully unlocked. Move to Pro when you want the full workflow suite, MCP server fleet, and Boss Agent orchestration. Team is for when you're running multiple agents across machines or collaborating with others.
Does Enterprise require a long-term commitment?
No. Enterprise is custom-scoped per project. Reach out to support@myaibrain.org and we'll design something that fits your deployment — whether that's a one-time license, annual contract, or usage-based arrangement.
